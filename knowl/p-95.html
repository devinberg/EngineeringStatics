We will normally work in the "plane of the page," that is, a 2-dimensional cartesian plane with a horizontal \(x\)-axis and a vertical \(y\)-axis. This coordinate system can represent either the front, side, or top view of a system as appropriate. In some problems it may be worthwhile to rotate the coordinate system, that is, to establish a coordinate system where the \(x\)-axis and \(y\)-axis are not longer horizontal and vertical. This is usually done to simplify the mathematics by avoiding simultaneous equations. See [provisional cross-reference: rotate coordinate system].
