Subsubsection A.1.4 PreTeXt tags

Here is a list of commonly used PreTeXt tags. Their purposes are mostly self evident.

For details on the use of tags see Chapter 3 of the [Author's Guide](https://pretextbook.org/doc/author-guide/html/overview.html).

  • <book>
  • <title>
  • <chapter>
  • <section>
  • <subsection>
  • <subsubsection>
  • <introduction>
  • <conclusion>
  • <objectives>
  • <example>
  • <exercises>
  • <exercise>
  • <hint>
  • <solution>
  • <aside>
  • <note>
  • <p> basic paragraph, like html
  • <paragraph> a paragraph with a run-in title
  • <em> emphasis, renders as italics
  • <figure> a numbered figure
  • <image> contains filename of associated image file

Mathematics is described in the Author's Guide

  • <m> inline math, uses LaTeX syntax, rendered with MathJax .
  • <me> single equation, centered
  • <md> display math, multiline
  • <men> numbered equation
  • <mdn> numbered display math

The XML Schema describes the PreTeXt vocabulary: the legal tags and how they nest. It's formal and confusing, so you may be better off looking at the source of documents that do what you want first. Another reference is and the Schema documentation which describes the schema
